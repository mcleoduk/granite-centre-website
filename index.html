About the Stone

Yes — granite is one of the most heat-resistant worktop materials available. You can place hot pans directly on the surface without causing damage. That said, we recommend using trivets as a habit, as sudden extreme temperature changes can, over time, affect the sealant.

Granite is a natural igneous rock — extremely hard, durable, and heat resistant. Each slab is unique. Marble is a metamorphic stone with elegant veining but is softer and more porous, making it better suited to lower-traffic areas. Quartz is an engineered stone made from crushed quartz and resin — highly consistent in pattern, non-porous, and very low-maintenance. We stock all three and can help you choose the right material for your project.

Yes. Granite is a natural stone with a slightly porous surface, so it benefits from sealing to protect against staining. We apply a professional-grade sealant as part of every installation. We recommend resealing every one to two years depending on use, which is a straightforward job you can do yourself at home.

Granite is a natural stone, so it does require more care than engineered alternatives. It needs sealing on installation and resealing every one to two years to stay stain-resistant — if left unsealed, it can absorb liquids and harbour bacteria over time. It's also heavier than composite materials, which occasionally requires additional cabinet support. And because every slab is unique, colour matching for extensions or repairs can be tricky. If low maintenance is your priority, quartz is worth considering — it's non-porous and requires no sealing. We stock both and are happy to help you weigh up the options for your project.

Granite is exceptionally hard and resistant to chipping under normal kitchen use. Chips can occur from a severe impact — typically a heavy object dropped directly onto a corner or edge. Cracks are rare but can happen if the worktop is poorly supported. Our installations are fully templated and properly supported to minimise this risk.
