Why Black Granite

The Advantages of Black Granite Worktops

Exceptional Durability

Granite scores 6–7 on the Mohs hardness scale — harder than most metals. Black granite worktops resist scratching, chipping, and daily wear with ease. A properly maintained surface will still look remarkable decades from now.

Timeless Aesthetics

Black stone has been used in architecture for centuries. It doesn't date, it doesn't trend — a black granite worktop is an investment in a kitchen that will still feel right in twenty years.

Heat Resistant

Granite can withstand temperatures far beyond anything a domestic kitchen produces. Place hot pans and baking trays directly on the surface — black granite won't crack, scorch, or discolour.

Versatile Design

Black granite pairs equally well with white shaker cabinetry, handleless grey units, dark wood, or industrial steel. It's one of the few worktop materials that works across virtually every kitchen style.

Easy to Maintain

A sealed black granite surface needs only warm soapy water for day-to-day cleaning. The dark colour is also practically forgiving — minor marks and everyday use are far less visible than on lighter stones.

Adds Property Value

Granite worktops add measurable value to a property. Black granite in particular is associated with premium kitchens — the kind of surface that features in estate agent photography and influences buyers.

Maintenance Guide

Caring for Your Black Granite Worktop

01

Seal on Installation — Reseal Every 1–2 Years

We apply a professional-grade impregnating sealant to every worktop we install. After 12–24 months, resealing is a simple DIY task using an off-the-shelf granite sealer — it takes around 30 minutes and makes a significant difference to long-term performance and stain resistance.

02

Daily Cleaning: Keep It Simple

Warm water and a small amount of washing-up liquid is all you need. Wipe down with a soft cloth or microfibre and dry thoroughly. Avoid abrasive pads, bleach, or vinegar-based cleaners — these can degrade the sealant over time.

03

Deal with Spills Promptly

Red wine, citrus juice, and cooking oils should be wiped up promptly rather than left to sit. A well-sealed surface handles these comfortably — it's only neglected sealant that causes lasting staining issues.

04

Use a Chopping Board

Granite is hard enough to blunt a kitchen knife. Repeated direct cutting can dull the polished finish over time. A chopping board protects both the worktop surface and your knives.
